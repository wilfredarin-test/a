Pipelines Division and CSRL signs MoA for Setting up 'Indian Oil Vidushi Centres

Mr. Gautam Ghosal, Executive Director (Human Resource) PHO, signed an MOA with Mr. S.K.Shahi, Director (CSRL) for setting up of "Indian Oil Vidushi" centres at Patna, Jaipur and Chennai, in esteemed presence of Mr. A.K. Singh, Director (Pipelines) on 5th August 2019.

IndianOil Vidushi project is a flagship CSR programme of IndianOil for empowering girls hailing from economically weaker section of society. Selected girls (Vidushis) are provided with specialized coaching and mentoring to succeed in JEE (Mains), JEE (Advanced) and other Examinations in India. The Project is a fully residential coaching program with selected Vidushis being provided with free coaching, accommodation, fooding, lodging, study materials etc. The project is a grand success with 46 out of 56 girls qualifying in JEE (Mains) during academic session of 2018-19.


Director's Voice

“The people who are crazy enough to think they can change the world are the ones who do.” –I strongly believe in these words of Steve Jobs.

I welcome all new members in team CSRL and wish to share my thoughts which are aligned to the above mentioned quote. We aim at achieving a mutual goal of creating excellence in anything that we do in the organisation, and this is only possible when we sincerely criticize our work and start learning from each one.

In the recent past, I have observed that following 7 challenges faced by CSRL are critical to our productivity. We all need to ponder on these and find a way out.


Less of Admissions in IITs & A-grade Institutions- This cause is disappointing our sponsors and associates. Going forward, we cannot focus just on the number of qualifications in JEE Mains and Advance but overall on admissions.

Weak Branding - We barely share the stories and news of our organisation on social media, we have yet not added our beneficiaries in ‘CSRL Alumni’ FB group which helps us create a brand of our own. With over 3000 ex- beneficiaries, we only have ~ 700 students in the group as of day.

High-Performance Management – We need to understand and work on High Performance Management, our work has to be time-bound as well as quality- bound.

Lacking commitment- We do work hard but we also have to take ownership. We need to work with passion and with commitment.

Adherence to Standards: We have shared a lot many policies of recruitment selection, safety; however we can do more in adhering to those. We need to understand that the policies are made for our benefits and keeping growth in mind. Quality, if not assured, jeopardizes the sustainability in long run.

Lack of innovation in selection – This is one of the most important determinants of success and so far our experienced PM(s) have seldom experimented in finding ways to select the best students in lesser expense. I am sure if all of us start thinking on this, we shall surely come with brilliant suggestions.

Lack of sense of urgency- We need to act now. The mantra for all of us. Go crazy and grow impatient in acheving the best.

However, I must admit here that I take pride in the strength of CSRL such as satisfaction of sponsors, manpower stability, and systems and processes in place to some extent, full support of the board and strong academics and all of You. I am confident that each of you can bring a change.

To conclude, we have set a vision of 2020, that is to achieve 175 admissions in IITs, 180 admissions in A- group Institutions (other than IITs), 250 admissions in NITs other than those listed in A group and 150 admissions in B grade institutions.

Let’s go get it!
