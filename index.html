Pipelines Division and CSRRL signs MoA for Setting up 'IndianOil Vidushi Centres'

Mr. Gautam Ghosal, Executive Director (Human Resource) PHO, signed an MOA with Mr. S.K.Shahi, Director (CSRL) for setting up of "Indian Oil Vidushi" centres at Patna, Jaipur and Chennai, in esteemed presence of Mr. A.K. Singh, Director (Pipelines) on 5th August 2019.

IndianOil Vidushi project is a flagship CSR programme of IndianOil for empowering girls hailing from economically weaker section of society. Selected girls (Vidushis) are provided with specialized coaching and mentoring to succeed in JEE (Mains), JEE (Advanced) and other Examinations in India. The Project is a fully residential coaching program with selected Vidushis being provided with free coaching, accommodation, fooding, lodging, study materials etc. The project is a grand success with 46 out of 56 girls qualifying in JEE (Mains) during academic session of 2018-19.


Director's Voice

On 29th July 2019, a meeting to discuss various challenges to CSRL and achieving a mutual goal of excellence was organised by Mr Shahi. All the staff of the Head Office was present at the meeting.The meeting intended to provide an overview of current challenges faced by CSRL and to involve everyone in setting targets for 2019-20.

Mr Shahi discussed nine major challenges that are our hampering the image and pace of productivity of the organisation. The challenges which were discussed during the meeting, were as follows:
  1. Rumours spread by Mr.Baleshwar Rai & Anand Kumar
  2. Admissions in IITs & A- grade Institutions
  3. Weak Branding
  4. High-Performance Management (Lacking commitment)
  5. Adherence to Standards: Poor
  6. Change in top management in PSUs
  7. Business Diversification
  8. Innovation in selection and
  9. Lack of sense of urgency

The strengths of CSRL were also discussed. The strengths that were highlighted and believed to take the organisation even farther are as follows: satisfaction of sponsors, near stable manpower, systems & processes in place up to some extent, full support of the board and strong academics.

In the second part of the meeting, everyone collectively focussed on the future view and discussed radical enhancement in the speed of action. Vision for 2019-2020 was set that we would try to achieve 175 admissions in IITs, 180 admissions in A- group Institutions (other than IITs), 250 admissions in NITs other than those listed in A group and 150 admissions in B grade institutions.

Later on, Centres which are on high alert and are to be put on review by the academic advisor on priority basis were highlighted. The centres are as follows: OMC BBS, Oil India Itanagar, NTPC & CONCOR-Varanasi, Kashmir, and IOC Patna & Noida.

Finally, the meeting ended with a discussion on readiness for 2019-20. The information on upcoming projects was given. The list of the upcoming project is as follows: IOC-Chennai, NFL-Chandigarh, ONGC- Tripura, IOC Petronas- Kolkata, and PLL- Delhi for Kashmir (Part of PLL- Kashmir Super 30). Mr Shahi motivated everyone to keep their belief stronger than steel. Everyone felt extremely encouraged and rolled up their sleeves to work efficiently for the newly set targets.
